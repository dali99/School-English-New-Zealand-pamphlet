History

Obviously both Australia and New Zealand share a lot of history, seeing as they are both in the the british commonwealth.

New Zealand was first discovered by the Polynesians in the start of the 14th century. The polynesians settled and became the indigenous people of New Zealand, the Maori.

New Zealand was then re-discovered by british sailor James Cook. Who was the first person to sail around, and map New Zealand. After that many people visited the Country, for trading, exploring, or maybe just to silence their ache for adventure!

In 1840 New Zealand joined the British Empir, with the treaty of Waitangi, giving Maori people "equal rights" with British citizens

Geography

New Zealand lies in Oceania, in the southern hemisphere. The climate is both cold and wet, similar to Norway, but perhaps their winters are a bit warmer.

The country is filled with beautiful mountains and canyons, green grass, and sheep, lots and lots of sheep. Seriously, there are more sheep in New Zealand than people. That's a lot of sheep.

In many ways New Zealand looks a lot like Norway, and their nature, is where most of their tourism comes from.

The largest mountain in New Zealand is Mount Cook, with a height of 3.724 meters.

As for the longest river, that's the Waikato river. And the Maori has long thought of it as a source for mana. Which is a fuel for magic, and pride.
